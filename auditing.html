Auditing

Auditing is defined as “the on-site verification activity, such as inspection or examination, of a process or quality system, to ensure compliance to requirements.”

Auditing is a mandatory requirement of most food technical standards and has many benefits including:

  • Confirms compliance with internal standards and those of your suppliers.
  • Helps drive continuous improvement by identifying strengths and weaknesses in your processes.
  • Assists in 3rd Party audit preparation
  • Provides reassurance to internal stakeholders in the business and increased confidence.

Tony has spent a considerable part of his career conducting audits of food manufacturing businesses throughout the UK and is a certified Lead Auditor.

  • Food Safety

    • This is an audit to verify the effectiveness of the food safety controls that are in place within your process and will focus on risks arising from physical, chemical, microbiological and allergenic hazards.

  • Legal Compliance

    • This audit will verify compliance with current food legislation relevant to your business and will follow a protocol similar to the periodic Competent Authority audits.

  • GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice)

    • This audit will verify compliance against food industry GMP observing both working practices and documentation.

  • Pre-Operational Inspection

    • An in-depth assessment of cleaning standards throughout the process, conducted pre-production, to verify the effectiveness of cleaning protocols in the removal of debris from food-contact surfaces and the processing environment.

  • Internal Quality Management System Audits

    • This will verify compliance with the companys’ internal quality system.

  • BRC/ISO Standard

    • An audit to verify compliance with the sections & clauses of the standard simulating the annual certification audit with a focus on both production facilities and documentation.

  • Vendor Assessment Audits/Service Provider

    • The effective management of both raw ingredient suppliers and service providers alike is critical to minimising or eliminating any risks originating from process inputs. This audit can take many forms depending on the risk presented by each individual supplier or service provider.

    This audit will verify compliance with vendor approval requirements.

  • Assured Standards

    • To verify compliance to the specific assurance standard eg. Bord Bia, RSPCA Assured, Red Tractor, SALSA etc.

  • Retailer/Customer Technical Standards

    • To verify compliance to the specific retailer/customer standard/directive/Code of Practice eg. PIU

  • Identity Preserved

    • To verify compliance to the specific origin designation and identity preserved requirements of the product.

Following completion, a comprehensive audit report will be produced detailing evidence of compliance & non-compliance with grading of the findings based on risk.

Audit duration will vary depending on the type & scope of the audit and the size & complexity of the business.

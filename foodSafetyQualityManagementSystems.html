Food Safety & Quality Management Systems

Quality Management Systems exist in many food businesses to create a structured approach to managing food safety and quality using detailed policies, procedures and working instructions to which the business operates.

The system may be tailored and certified to a specific GFSI (Global Food Safety Initiative) scheme eg. BRC or one of the many other assurance schemes available such as Bord Bia, SALSA (Safe and Local Supplier Approval) etc. or alternatively may be uncertified and bespoke to the particular food business. Tesco have developed their own unique technical standard – TFMS (Tesco Food Manufacturing Standard) to which all suppliers must be compliant.

The type of QMS that a business chooses to implement (and whether or not this is certified) will largely be dictated by the nature of the business and its customer base. Many of the world’s largest food retailers will mandate supplier certification to a GFSI scheme whereas may smaller processors or food service businesses may opt for SALSA approval. For others a bespoke QMS will suffice.

For businesses without a current QMS:

NFS can:

  • Hold an initial discussion with senior management to agree the scope and type of QMS required.
  • Conduct a detailed gap analysis to review existing systems, documentation, procedures and working practices etc. and generate a plan for the phased implementation of the QMS.
  • Project manage the complete implementation of a QMS compliant with the requirements of any GFSI scheme eg. BRC or any other 3rd Party assurance scheme eg. ISO, Bord Bia, SALSA etc. from the initial phase through to final approval.
  • Deliver QMS awareness training to all levels within the business.
  • Draft all pertinent documentation.
  • Conduct a pre-approval audit and assist the business in addressing all corrective actions prior to final certification.
  • Attend the 3rd Party assessment audit as Technical Representative for the business.
For businesses with an existing certified QMS:

NFS can:

  • Streamline/restructure/upgrade bureaucratic or antiquated systems, rationalising superfluous detail to improve manageability whilst ensuring full compliance to legal, customer and certification requirements.
  • Provide ongoing periodic technical support for the maintenance of the QMS to ensure subsequent approval audits are successful.
  • Conduct periodic internal audits.
BRC/ISO Updates

Periodic updates to standards or assurance schemes will require the business to undertake a gap analysis and implement new and/or amend existing procedures, documentation and working practices to gain approval to the new version of the updated standard or assurance scheme.

NFS can:

  • Provide full transition services to enable the business to move seamlessly from the old to the new standard or assurance scheme.
Sector Specific/ Identity Preserved Standards

Many companies achieve certification to sector specific or identity preserved standards such as Red Tractor Assured, RSPCA Assured, Soil Association etc.

NFS can:

  • Guide your business through the entire certification process to final approval.
Irish Catering and Retail Food Businesses

Compliance with Irish and EU legislation governing food hygiene can be demonstrated by food business operators with certification to two Irish standards:

  • I.S. 340:2007 Hygiene in Catering
  • I.S. 341:2007 Hygiene in Food Retailing and Wholesaling.

NFS can:

  • Develop and implement a QMS that is compliant with the requirements of I.S. 340:2007 or I.S. 341:2007 and guide your business through the entire certification process to final FSAI approval.
